Diagnosis Pathway

Systematic approach to diagnosing heart failure in Primary Care

Defining Heart Failure

A clinical syndrome with typical symptoms/signs caused by structural and/or functional cardiac abnormality, resulting in a reduced cardiac output or elevated intracardiac pressures at rest/stress

ESC HF guidelines, EHJ 2016; 37:2129

Key Point: Demonstration of an underlying cardiac cause is central to the diagnosis of HF

Suspected New Diagnosis - Diagnostic Pathway

Step 1

Initial Assessment & Investigations

Following history and clinical examination, without delay refer for:

Blood Tests:

  • NTproBNP (primary diagnostic biomarker)
  • FBC, U&E, LFTs, TFTs, HbA1c

ECG

Management: Start a loop diuretic to relieve symptoms and consider a CXR as long as it doesn't delay treatment

Step 2

NTproBNP Results & Referral Pathway

< 400 ng/L

HF Unlikely

Consider alternative diagnosis

400-2000 ng/L

Refer to HF Services

Specialist review of echo within 6 weeks

> 2000 ng/L

Urgent Referral

Specialist assessment and echo within 2 weeks

Referral Method: Refer urgently to HF services via e-consultation or electronic referral system (ERS) if NTproBNP ≥ 400 ng/L

Step 3

Echo Results & HF Classification

HFrEF

EF ≤ 40%

Proceed to HFrEF treatment pathway

HFmrEF

EF 41-49%

Proceed to HFmrEF treatment pathway

HFpEF

EF ≥ 50%

Proceed to HFpEF treatment pathway

Key Diagnostic Reminders

  • Clinical features alone have poor diagnostic accuracy - biomarkers are essential
  • Start loop diuretics for symptom relief while awaiting diagnostic results
  • Urgent referral for NTproBNP > 2000 ng/L (assessment within 2 weeks)
  • Demonstration of underlying cardiac cause is central to HF diagnosis
