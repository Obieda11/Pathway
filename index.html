Evidence-based guidance for Primary Care professionals

Introduction

Heart failure affects over a million people across the UK with 200,000 new diagnoses every year. It is a life-limiting condition that too often causes emergency hospital admissions, poor quality of life and ultimately early death.

It is possible to live well with heart failure and our Heart Failure pathways have been standardised for use in Primary Care services to facilitate referrals to diagnostic and heart failure treatment services.

Early diagnosis of people with heart failure with prompt access to integrated services and specialist care can help to cut emergency admissions, improve quality of life and give people the opportunity to live well and longer.

Key Statistic: 80% of heart failure is diagnosed in hospital yet 40% of people had symptoms that should have triggered an earlier assessment in Primary Care in the months prior to hospital admission.

Types of Heart Failure

HFrEF

Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction

EF ≤ 40%

Weakened heart muscle can't squeeze effectively (Systolic HF)

HFmrEF

Heart Failure with Mildly Reduced Ejection Fraction

EF 41-49%

Intermediate category with some reduction in pumping ability

HFpEF

Heart Failure with Preserved Ejection Fraction

EF ≥ 50%

Stiff heart muscle can't relax normally (Diastolic HF)

Important Considerations

  • Individualised Care: Investigation and management must always be individualised taking into account frailty, palliative and end of life considerations and the patient's wishes.
  • Frailty Awareness: Many heart failure patients will be living with frailty. Consider risk of fall/fracture/electrolyte disturbances.
  • Recent Advances: Recent advances in pharmacological treatment have expanded options for patients, aligned with ESC guidelines.
